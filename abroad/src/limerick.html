Welcome to Ireland!

My first week in Ireland.

After a day and a half of travelling, I am finally in Ireland! It was an uncommonly sunny and dry day, so the view as the plane was landing was spectacular! The pictures do not do the country enough justice; Ireland is truly very green! After collecting my bags and passing through customs, I boarded the bus that would take us to the campus. The ride was about 45 minutes and we got to see some of the surrounding areas near Limerick.

Finally, at about 7 pm I reached Thomond Village, my home for the next 5 months. When I walked into the kitchen, I met four of my roommates who immediately asked me where I was from. Apparently, they had made a bet on where the next roommate would be coming from and some had correctly guessed that I would be an American! For our first dinner, we all ate spaghetti and salad, as all of the restaurants and shops were closed. This is a surprise to me, as in the US many stores are open 7 days a week, and are open until 11 pm. It was quite funny to see my French roommates cutting up and eating their spaghetti with a knife and fork! I had never seen anyone eat spaghetti like this but I must say it is very mannerly. My roommates also always say "have a good meal" or "enjoy your meal" before anyone eats. This really isn't a thing in the US so I was quite surprised that they always say this, but I also think it's very polite.

Another great thing about living with other international students is that we all speak different languages. Since four of my roommates are French, there's a lot of French spoken in our apartment. I have even learnt some basic phrases in French (Je m'appelle Vivian. Je suis une americaine. J'habite en Caroline du Sud), although now I regret not taking French in high school! My roommates also often ask me to read over emails or assignments in English for them. Doing this really forces me to think about language and how to explain grammar rules I don't really think about. From this, I can see how many consider English a very difficult language!

My roommates often tell me that they find the Irish accent hard to understand. I understand most Irish people well, but there are a few where even I struggle to process their speech. There's a girl in one of my classes who talks so fast that it takes me at least a minute to understand and respond. I hope she doesn't think I'm dumb or something. It's just that in America we talk way slower.

In the next couple of days, we did a bit of exploring on campus and attended orientation modules. I was really impressed at how modern the campus looks but also about how much natural beauty there is. I love getting to cross the Shannon River everyday on my way to classes. We also learnt how to ride the bus system and went on a walking tour of the city and a trip to the mall. To me, Limerick is unlike any place I've seen before! It is both small and big at the same time; and also modern and old at the same time too. I love the Riverwalk area and how King John's Castle is sitting there admist all of the much newer buildings in the city. Although it is quite cold, it is very enjoyable to walk around in the city and just get lost in it. Coming from a place that is more sprawled out and requires a car to get around, I appreciate the walkability of Limerick.

Cyrus and I also tried out the Irish McDonald's. While the food was ok, we both agreed that it was quite expensive compared to back home. Things in Ireland tend to be a bit more expensive, with the exception of dairy and meat (which taste way better here). I also have to mind the conversion between the dollar and the euro.

Another thing I'll have to get used to are the small fees everywhere. There are many things to do regarding signing up for classes. Unfortunately, I learnt that the classes I had selected before coming here would not all fit into my schedule. Unlike at an American university, there is only ever one section of a class happening here at UL. Because two of my courses were held at the same time, I had to drop one of them. In the end, I ended up taking Data Structures and Algorithms, Introduction to Web Development, Performance Technology, and Irish Traditional Music and Dance.

All in all, there is much more to write about, especially about my travels in Ireland, so see you in the next post!
