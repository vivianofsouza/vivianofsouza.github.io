Cork + The Blarney Castle

Ancient Ruins and City Sights.

This week, we went to Blarney Castle and Cork City. Blarney Castle is famous for its Blarney Stone. Legend has it that if you can kiss it you'll be granted the gift of eloquence. There are many castles in Ireland and this is one of the most famous.

When we got there, we walked through the visitor's center and the gardens. As we approached the entrance of the castle, we saw two pillories, places where criminals would have to sit as punishment with their heads and arms out. When we went into the castle, one of the first things that we saw was a trap door. If someone pulled a lever, the person standing on the door would fall into this really deep hole and be imprisoned there.

Then, we explored the inside of the castle. Most of the castle was accessible by very narrow staircases that had only a tiny rope to hang on as a railing. I'm not usually scared of heights, but I was a bit afraid of missing a step and falling onto the throng of people behind me.

When we reached the top floors, we saw the rooms for the kitchen, the kids' bedroom, and quarters of the Lord and Lady of the house. We even wandered into the bathroom of the castle! Thankfully no bad odors from centuries ago could be smelled.

Finally, we ended up in the Great Hall where the castle's inhabitants would have held banquets and dances. Here we also got to kiss the Blarney Stone. To kiss the stone, you have to lay on your back with the guide holding you, while you slide back and crane your neck to reach the stone. Since I am on the short side, I was too scared to go all the way down, so I just touched the wall instead.

After touring the castle, we went to the Poison Garden. Everything in this garden is poisonous, so we could only look but not touch the plants. We then stood on the walls of the castle and caught the view of the river and countryside beyond.

Around lunchtime, we took the bus into Cork. We only got to spend 2 hours there so we made the most of it by visiting the English Market. The English Market is a very big farmer's and artisan market. There are so many things you can buy, such as fresh seafood, meats, cheeses, breads, and other foods like jams and teas. I bought a bag of Pistachio Truffle tea from a shop as a souvenir for my mom since she loves drinking tea. I also bought a nice-looking raspberry cheesecake from a bakery stall. I hope to come back to the market again because they were so many stalls and I want to buy some more things for my family and friends back at home.

On our way back to the bus we wandered around and took in the sights of the city. Cork is the second-largest city in Ireland, and it definitely felt much bigger than Limerick. We did not spend enough time here and I would like to come back sometime soon!
